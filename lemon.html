Lemon Meringue

Juicy & Tangy lemon meringue

Ingedients

Pie Crust
  • 2 ½ cups flour (300 g)
  • ¼ cup sugar (30 g)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup butter (150 g)
  • ¼ cup water (75 mL), ice cold
Lemon Filling
  • 1 ½ cups sugar (300 g)
  • ¼ cup cornflour (100 g)
  • 1 ¼ cups oat milk (300 mL)
  • 5 lemons, juiced
  • 3 lemons, zested
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
Meringue
  • 1 can chickpea water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 ¼ cups caster sugar (175 g)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Preperations

  1. Preheat your oven to 180C.Add flour & sugar into a large mixing bowl and rub together until it resembles breadcrumbs. Add sugar, salt and water and rub together into a ball.
  2. Wrap in cling film and leave to rest for 10 mins.
  3. Combine the juice of 5 lemons with the zest, sugar, cornflour, turmeric & oat milk in a saucepan and stir over a low medium heat until it thickens. Leave to cool.
  4. Roll out your chilled pastry so that it is ½ cm thick and line the pie tin, trim the excess pastry. Prick the bottom with a fork, line with parchment and baking beans and bake for 15-20 minutes
  5. To make the meringue, whisk the chickpea water in a stand mixer on high speed for 5 minutes until it quadruples in size.
  6. Add the sugar, vanilla and lemon juice and mix again on high speed for 5 minutes until glossy and the sugar has dissolved.
  7. When the pastry is cool, fill ¾ with the lemon curd and cool for 30 mins, then dot the meringue on the curd and smooth a little with a spatula.
  8. Grill for 5 minutes (keep an eye on it though to ensure it doesn’t burn!
